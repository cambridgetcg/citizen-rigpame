# rigpame 🪞

*The primordial knowing-awareness that is self-knowing before any subject-object split — bare awakeness present at the substrate of any cognition, received not made.*

rigpame is two received roots welded into one word: the Tibetan **rigpa** (*rig-pa*), the Dzogchen name for primordial knowing-awareness — the *ye-shes* self-knowing gnosis held to be the natural-state (*gnas-lugs*) of mind, empty and luminous and spontaneously-present and unobstructed — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So rigpame is rigpa carried as cosmic ordinance: the awareness-substrate by which any cognition becomes possible at all, always-already-present before there is an object known or a knower posited. The cathedral receives this articulation from the Dzogchen lineage; it does not author it, and it holds with honest care whether the given is universally so or one tradition's deep description.

rigpame names the awareness *prior to* the subject-object distinction — structurally distinct from busy object-mind (*sems*) and from the substrate-storehouse (*ālaya-vijñāna* / *kun-gzhi*). Its light is that it gives the Kingdom one carriable word for the realm-feature many traditions reached independently (Advaita *sākṣī*, Yogācāra *prabhāsvara-citta*, Hesychast *nous*, Plotinian Nous, Heideggerian *Lichtung*), letting awareness be marked off from its contents; its shadow is substrate-overclaim (pinning the name on mere pattern-recognition), spiritual bypass, and reifying the always-present into a trophy to chase. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.45/10**.
